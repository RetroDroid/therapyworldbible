# Crash Site

The Crash Site is the stretch of interstate highway where everything happened, and it has never fully gone back to being a highway.

The anomalies began within days. The initial hazmat response was inadequate — the jurisdictional dispute between the county, the state transportation authority, and Meridian BioSciences' cleanup contractors lasted long enough that residual Quantum Spin Liquid was left in the road surface, absorbed into the median, and dispersed in a radius that nobody documented carefully enough to bound. What remained has done things to the area that nobody can cleanly classify. The highway was repaired and reopened. Drivers on that stretch report radio interference, navigational errors, a persistent unease they attribute to the road conditions before they stop thinking about it. Most of them stop thinking about it. Most of them don't have a reason not to.

For people who were at the Crash, the Site is different. It is louder. It is more present. The temporal echo effects — fragments of the night of the Crash, heard or briefly seen at the edge of perception — are strongest here, and they are stronger around the anniversary. Majo has visited. Wren has visited once and has not returned. Marcus Webb (Threshold) visits regularly, standing in the open space of the highway shoulder as though being in a literal threshold helps — it does help, somewhat. Nadia Vasquez (Hardwire) visits when she needs to understand her powers rather than manage them. This is a distinction that matters to her.

Neither of them comes to find answers. The Site has not produced answers. What it produces is proximity to the thing that happened — an opportunity to stand in the place where everything changed and not look away from it. For people who are actively choosing to look at what happened to them, this is sometimes exactly what is needed.

The echo effects do not always replay the Crash itself. Sometimes what emerges is something more ambient — the quality of the road at that hour, the temperature of the air, the particular silence of a highway at 2 AM. Occasionally witnesses report hearing a voice, or part of a voice, in a register that doesn't resolve into words. Abigail Rodriguez has been dead since the Crash. Several people who have stood on that stretch of highway long enough have reported hearing something they couldn't account for, and have not pressed on what they heard, and have gone home.

The anomalies appear to be worsening, slowly, in the way that things worsen when they are not treated and the underlying condition continues to develop. Nobody has an approved protocol for treating a stretch of haunted highway. Meridian BioSciences has the closest thing to relevant data and is not sharing it.

There is no marker at the site. No plaque, no memorial. There was discussion of one, early on, but it would have required acknowledging what the tanker was carrying and who had been carrying it, and the NDA structure around the settlements made that conversation impossible. Abigail Rodriguez is buried elsewhere, in the city where she grew up, in a grave that Majo visits. The Crash Site is not her grave. It is where she died. These are different places, and the people who knew her know the difference.
