# Alliance Headquarters

The Alliance Headquarters is a ground-level facility beneath the Waterfowl Migration Museum in Greenville's river district — a separate space from the Gander, which floats cloaked above the city, and which is Andrew's. The Headquarters is the Alliance's. The distinction matters to Andrew. He has not explained why to his teammates, but it involves the difference between a place you make for yourself and a place you make for other people, and he has made exactly one of each.

The entrance is through the museum — specifically, through a door marked CONSERVATION STORAGE at the back of the ground floor, which requires a keycard, a six-digit code, and the correct weight pressure on the third stair of the staircase that descends from it. Andrew designed the entry sequence to be annoying enough that nobody stumbles through it accidentally. It has worked. The museum's two docents have never questioned why the storage area has so much security. They are paid well and have, each of them, independently concluded that Andrew Wolfe is peculiar in ways that are not their business.

The facility itself is functional and undecorated. Andrew designed the layout; the Alliance members furnished it collectively, which means it has the particular character of a space nobody owns. There is a long central table, a communications station that ties into the city emergency network, a display wall for mapping and briefings, and a small kitchen that contains, at any given time: coffee, oat milk that belongs to Arrowhead and is labeled, a container of something from James Webb's kitchen that Red Lightning brought over and that everyone has eaten without asking what it is, and whatever Mosquito Man has left behind on his last visit, which is usually something from a vending machine and is usually already half-gone.

There are six workstations. Rock Woman uses hers for geological monitoring — she has a feed running to seismic sensors she installed at six points around the city, which she checks on a schedule that maps roughly to whenever she remembers to. Red Lightning uses his to run incident modeling at speeds the display wall can barely keep up with. Arrowhead has calibrated theirs so the lighting matches their preferred conditions for reviewing tactical footage, which took three conversations with Andrew and a weekend of adjustment. Duckman's station is the most organized. Nobody has touched it in his absence, because the organization is legible enough that moving anything would be obvious.

The Headquarters has been used for full Alliance briefings four times. It has been used for informal two-person working sessions far more often. It has been used, once, as a place where Red Lightning sat alone for several hours after a particularly disorienting period of temporal memory cross-contamination, because it was a room that felt like the present and he needed that. Nobody asked about it. He has not forgotten that nobody asked.

There is a corkboard on the wall near the kitchen that has been there since the beginning. Andrew put it up as a working board. It has, over time, accumulated things that are not operational: a photograph from the founding; a note in Rock Woman's handwriting that says *Tuesday: seismic event, minor, nothing* in her careful script; a hand-drawn map of Detroit that is clearly Mosquito Man's work and which nobody has removed because it is, in its unsanctioned way, very good.

Andrew designed this place for a team. The team uses it. He considers this, privately, one of the better outcomes of a particular decade of his life.
