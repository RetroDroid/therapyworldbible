# THE NAME
### A Lazaretto Script — No. 3

*He has been through these documents many times. They reward multiple readings. What is visible on a first pass is different from what becomes visible on a fourth, a seventh, a twelfth — not because the documents change, but because reading deeply enough acquaints you with the architecture of what was being concealed. You learn the shapes that concealment makes. You learn where to look for the load-bearing thing.*

## PAGE ONE (4 PANELS)

**PANEL 1**

*Wide establishing shot. A back room in a building that has been several things — formerly a clinic, currently an administrative office for a nonprofit that does not ask too many questions about who uses the storage room after hours. The room is small and spare: a table, two chairs, a power outlet. The kind of space that accumulates without being attended to.*

*REUBEN MARSH (LAZARETTO) at the table. He is alone. The documents are spread around him with the precision of someone who has been working through them systematically across weeks and months — organized by batch, by document type, by chain of authority. He knows this material. He is inside it again.*

*CAPTION: He has been through these documents many times. They reward multiple readings. What is visible on a first pass is different from what becomes visible on a fourth, a seventh, a twelfth — not because the documents change, but because reading deeply enough acquaints you with the architecture of what was being concealed. You learn the shapes that concealment makes.*

**PANEL 4**

*Close on his power reading the documents. Not actualization — just perception. The latent weight in paper that contains a buried account. Not kinetic potential, not destructive energy, but something his power finds anyway: the specific weight of what is being held down, what has been deliberately kept from surfacing. The paper is heavy with it.*

*CAPTION: His power reads objects for their destructive potential. What it finds in these documents is not what it was built to find. The paper is ordinary paper. The weight in it is not kinetic. It is the weight of an account that was buried carefully, by people with resources specifically allocated to the burying, in a system that accommodated the burial. His power reads it anyway. The paper is heavy.*

## PAGE FOUR (FULL PAGE)

*The page. Close enough that the specific entry is fully legible.*

*The entry for Rodriguez, A. is unremarkable on its face — a line in a list, the specific clinical formatting of a casualty report. One line among several. The language is neutral in the way that official language is neutral when it is describing something that should not have been neutral at all.*

*His hand is on the table beside the page. Still.*

*CAPTION: He has read this page before. He has been through this section twice, on two different passes through the documents, and he read the names and the outcomes and continued. The accounting was clear. He knew there was a fatality. He was working toward the people who had caused it. He continued.*

*CAPTION: Tonight he does not continue.*

*CAPTION: He did not have her name attached to a thesis about the contamination her killers made. He has it now.*

## PAGE NINE (FULL PAGE)

*The room from above. Reuben at the table, the documents spread around him, the overhead light. Small. Spare. The particular solitude of this work — no one watching, no one waiting, no community for the carrying of what he is carrying.*

*The documents on the table: the board memo, the margin calculation, the chain of authority, the incident report, the claims filing, the settlement ledger. Every page is part of the same account. Every page was produced by a system that was operating correctly, by its own terms, when it produced the outcome.*

*CAPTION: He is not a reliable narrator of his own impact. He knows this about himself. He has been told it, has told it to himself, has examined it carefully in the available quiet of his own accounting. He knows what he is: a person who perceives the latent destructive potential in things and does something with it, who cannot stop fighting for people who are afraid of him and has in fact never learned how, who told a man he had never met that he was going to fight for him whether the man wanted it or not, and meant it as a reassurance.*

*CAPTION: He is going to fight for Abigail Rodriguez. He has not said this yet. It is not a decision he makes with a speech or a moment of resolve, because he is not the kind of person who makes decisions that way. It is a fact that the room now contains, the way the room contains the documents and the table and the overhead light and his power quietly reading every object in it.*

*CAPTION: He has not known her. He has found her in a document. He has her name and her age and her degree program and the number that Apex's lawyers decided was the correct amount. He has the specific irony of her research subject. He has the fact that she was in the northbound car and was the only fatality and has been a line in a settlement ledger under nondisclosure for years, her name and her thesis and whatever warmth she moved through the world with available to no one who has been looking for accountability.*

*CAPTION: He is going to fight for her. He is going to fight for the specific dollar figure that Apex put on her life and find the people who signed off on it and continue the work he has been doing and hold what he is holding toward the people who should be holding it and are not.*

*CAPTION: Whether she would have wanted this: he does not know. He has thought about this, across months of working toward people who did not ask him to work toward them, and he does not know. He is not going to stop. He has established this about himself in the only way he establishes anything — from the evidence, from what the facts support — and the fact is that he is not going to stop whether she would have wanted it, whether the fighting produces anything she would have recognized as justice, whether anyone thanks him or benefits or even knows.*

## PAGE ELEVEN (FULL PAGE)

*The room. Reuben Marsh, alone in it, the documents in order on the table, the overhead light, the locked door, the storage boxes against the wall.*

*He is not moving yet. He will return to these documents tomorrow. He will return to the work. He will continue in the direction he has been continuing, and the name he found tonight will be specific and weighted where before the fatality was abstract, and the weight of it will be present in the room with every subsequent page he reads.*

*CAPTION: She was twenty-four. She was studying their contamination. She argued, warmly and without resolution, about whether individual heroism mattered against systemic problems. Someone in the car was usually on the other side of that argument. Neither of them was right. Neither of them needed to be.*

*CAPTION: She was being taken home first.*

*CAPTION: He puts the documents in order. He will come back tomorrow.*

*CAPTION: He is going to fight for her.*
