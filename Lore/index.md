# Lore

- [[Quantum Spin Liquid]] — The experimental compound synthesized by Meridian BioSciences that spilled in the Crash. Grants unique powers by amplifying latent neurological capacities, with individualized side effects that worsen existing vulnerabilities. Residual compound continues to warp the Crash Site.
- [[The Crash - Full Account]] — The full narrative of the 2:17 AM collision: two cars, a stalled tanker, and the spill that changed everything. Abigail Rodriguez dies. Everyone else survives into something they didn't choose.
- [[Timeline]] — A working chronology from Rock Woman's prehistoric origins through the Crash, its aftermath, and the open questions that remain.
- [[Winter Is Dead]] — On grief and Abigail Rodriguez: the river she studied, the love that doesn't resolve, what Sarah chose, and what it means to stand under cherry blossoms in the spring after the first winter.
- [[Three Points Where Two Lines Meet]] — On the triangle made by Poe, Lazaretto, and Lectric: the canon, the ripe and ruin, Reuben's ritual counting and upward prayer, the texture of Poe's presence in Ward L and the sound the sea makes, the tessellation that closes between him and Poe, Alexander's specific unbearable position at the third point, and the fourth point — the entity who moves in the dark at the edge of the shape and breaks it. Poe smiles on Lazaretto. She frowns on Lectric. She does not intervene for either. The triangle holds. And in the dark at the edge of it, something else is moving.
- [[Midnight Pretenders]] — On the cosmic relationship between Snare, Lectric, and Poe, through the lens of Tomoko Aran's 1983 song. Alexander and Snare are midnight pretenders together — he performs trustworthiness, she is the trap that presents as a threshold. The night sea is the level where his reaching happens and does not arrive. The chorus is his compulsion: to monopolize what cannot be accumulated. The deep sea bottom is where Snare is, receiving the words that cannot be said. The warmth in the dark is real and is also the mechanism of the trap. Poe holds the sea. She knows where the boat is going. She is not saying.
- [[The Northbound Car]] — On Nadia Vasquez, Hardwire, the bystander the crash accounting doesn't have a category for: the northbound driver who took the residue home and has been managing it alone since. Her powers surge with emotion; she routes the charge through empirical discipline and through returning to the Crash Site, where the compromised infrastructure accepts what she gives it. Through the lenses of four songs — about losing self-control, about the city at three in the morning, about whether a constructed self is a real self, and about the seraphim who never breaks character — an account of what it costs to be consistently right there when no one is watching and no one is going to say so.
