# Timeline

A working timeline for the Therapy universe. Not every beat is fully canonized. Items marked with [?] are open questions. Ages given are at the time of the event unless otherwise noted.

---

## Deep Past

- Rock Woman comes into being. The exact date is unknown, including to her. Her earliest clear memories predate recorded human civilization — the weight of glaciers, the particular silence of a world without cities.
- Rock Woman is present during the Roman Empire, the Black Death, various colonial periods. She watches. She intervenes rarely.
- Rock Woman accumulates wealth across centuries: real estate held across generations, gold pulled from the earth before anyone thought to regulate it, strategic patience in investments that compound differently when you don't die.
- Rock Woman changes her form to a woman, takes the name Alice Brown, and begins operating in the modern world as a geological consultant, finding ore deposits and predicting fault behavior.
- Alice Brown joins what will become the Justice Alliance in its early formation.

## Before the Crash

- **Reuben Marsh, age 17**, is in a grocery store during an argument with his mother when the latent destructive potential in a display of canned goods briefly actualizes. Three people are treated for lacerations. Reuben is taken into custody. Nobody asks if he is all right.
  - He is placed in a containment facility — billed as a treatment program, operating as something else. The ward has a designation. Staff call it the lazaretto.
  - Over five years of confinement, Reuben builds a philosophy from the available material: systems of authority protect themselves, not people. The law is a costume the powerful wear when it fits and shed when it doesn't.
  - At the furthest edge of his power — the edge he finds in the long hours of containment — he begins to perceive things that are present without being visible. The lazaretto is a threshold space. It has properties.
  - She finds him there. Not on any schedule he can predict. Sometimes the room shifts in a way that has nothing to do with his power and nothing to do with the institution, and she is present.
- **Reuben Marsh, age 22**, is released. He takes the name Lazaretto.
- **Red Lightning, age 23**, runs faster than the speed of light for the first time in the year 2170, and is propelled backward through time. He arrives in the present day, landing in Detroit. He locates his great-great-great-grandfather James Webb — a 60-year-old retired electrician in Detroit's Corktown neighborhood — and begins building the containment suit that makes his powers safe to use. James has handled the revelation with remarkable practicality. He keeps a separate notebook for "things I should probably not know" and does not open it often. Red Lightning enters therapy shortly after arriving.
- **Mathew Whitney, age 15**, is bitten by an enchanted mosquito in his northeast Detroit neighborhood. He develops flight and wall-adhesion. He designs and builds articulated mechanical wings as deliberate misdirection — anyone who sees him assumes the wings are the source of the flight — and a sonic disarmament device he calls the buzzer. He begins street-level heroics. His parents think he is in robotics club.
- **Andrew Wolfe's parents** are killed on his thirteenth birthday during a robbery at a Greenville restaurant. The investigation reveals the robbery was orchestrated by a crime organization rooted in the city's underfunded neighborhoods. Andrew does not miss this detail. He spends the next decade building Wolfe Holdings into a functional operation, designing the Gander, and becoming the vigilante protector of Greenville. He is never entirely certain whether he is a hero or someone who needs to feel like one.
- **The Justice Alliance forms** from independently operating heroes: Duckman, Mosquito Man, Rock Woman, Red Lightning, and Arrowhead. The arrangement becomes official in the way things become official when nobody formally stops them.
- **Michelle MacDuffie's** music career peaks. She has built it over fifteen years — not an overnight ascent but a sustained, deliberate accumulation of work, reputation, and audience. Her son **Jackson**, 14, begins experiencing early signs of unusual spatial awareness: glimpses of alternate realities at the edges of his perception. Nobody knows what this is yet. Jackson doesn't tell anyone.
- **Majo Majima** (26), **Sarah Florence**, and **Abigail Rodriguez** (24) are in a polyamorous relationship. Majo and Sarah had been partners before Abigail entered the picture; they broke up and stayed close in the specific way people do when they're too entangled to separate cleanly. Abigail is the center of gravity that keeps all three in stable orbit.
- **Trinity May Grace**, 16, and **Jackson MacDuffie**, 14, are together before the Crash. [?] An accident involving Michelle MacDuffie leaves Trinity mute and traumatized and afraid of her. Michelle carries this. The details are not discussed in group sessions. The shape of it is known.
- **Majo and Sarah become ex-partners**. They remain close. Abigail is still the center.

## Year Zero: The Crash

**2:17 AM. A stretch of interstate highway outside the city. The road is dry. Visibility is adequate. Weather is not a factor.**

- An **Apex Haul Logistics** tanker carrying Quantum Spin Liquid for **Meridian BioSciences** has stalled on the highway median due to a mechanical failure that driver **Dale Pryce** (12-year employee, clean record) reported to dispatch earlier that evening. Dispatch did not pull the route. The truck sits partially blocking both lanes — enough to matter, not enough to be visible at speed until it is too late.
- **Car One (southbound):** Michelle MacDuffie (38) is driving. Diana Rice (Dangerous) is in the front passenger seat. In the backseat, Jackson (14) and Trinity (16) have fallen asleep together. Michelle and Diana were talking. Neither will say what they were talking about.
- **Car Two (northbound):** Majo Majima (26) is driving, taking Abigail home first. Sarah Florence and Abigail Rodriguez (24) are in the passenger seats. Sarah and Abigail are arguing in the way people argue when they love each other — gently, the kind of disagreement that would have been forgotten by morning. Nobody remembers now what the argument was about.
- Michelle's car strikes the tanker first, on the passenger side. Car Two hits moments later, from the opposite direction. The tanker ruptures. Quantum Spin Liquid spreads across three lanes of highway.
- **Abigail Rodriguez dies on impact.** She is 24. She is the only fatality.
- **Car Three (northbound):** Nadia Vasquez (31), a construction worker heading home after a night shift, is close enough behind Car Two that she cannot stop in time. She does not strike the tanker directly. She drives into the spill. The Quantum Spin Liquid covers the front of her car and reaches her through the windshield. She pulls over. She helps where she can until paramedics arrive. Nobody at the scene knows yet what the compound is or what it will do.
- **Marcus Webb** (34), a paramedic, is among the first responders. While pulling survivors from one of the vehicles, he is drenched in Quantum Spin Liquid from the ruptured tanker.
- **Alexander Poe** (Lectric) is not in either car. His account: he was driving behind Michelle's car and stopped when he saw the collision. He was exposed to Quantum Spin Liquid through contact with the road surface while approaching the scene. The mechanism of his exposure is the most disputed element of any survivor's account. The compound was concentrated at the point of impact; the distance he claims to have been standing from it is inconsistent with the exposure he would have needed. Nobody has been able to prove anything. Lectric has not volunteered clarification.
- **The Crash Site** becomes anomalous due to residual Quantum Spin Liquid never properly cleaned from the scene — the result of jurisdictional disputes, inadequate hazmat response, and a containment failure in the early hours after the incident.
- The police report lists the cause as a multi-vehicle collision due to an unlighted road obstruction. The tanker's contents are documented as "industrial chemical compound, transport manifest on file." The manifest is sealed under a Meridian BioSciences confidentiality agreement and has not been made public.
- Apex Haul Logistics' liability is settled out of court, with non-disclosure agreements attached to every settlement. Meridian BioSciences issues a statement about cooperating with investigators and does not discuss the incident publicly again.
- **Dale Pryce** is alive. He has no powers. He was just a man in a truck that his company should have pulled from the road, and none of what followed is in proportion to what he did or didn't do, and he knows it, and it doesn't help.

## Year Zero to One: Manifestation

- **Quantum Spin Liquid exposure** begins the process of granting powers to the Crash survivors. The process is not immediate or consistent.
- **Michelle MacDuffie** develops invisibility and forcefields. The forcefields' side effect — they silence everything said behind them — is deeply metaphorical, and she has spent significant time in therapy not discussing this. The more immediate consequence is the paranoia: the compound finds something in her neurological architecture and amplifies it into a clinical condition. She becomes unable to go on stage without believing the audience can see through her. She cancels tours. Her management describes it as burnout. It is not burnout.
- **Diana Rice** develops mind control: specific, reliable, and deeply effective against people who are emotionally compromised. The side effect is that her own inhibitions are disabled — not reduced, disabled. What Diana wants, she pursues. The internal friction that most people have, the voice that says *not this, not now, not this way* — she doesn't have that anymore.
- **Jackson MacDuffie** (14) develops teleportation. He can displace himself and others through space with precision that is still sharpening. Under stress or confinement, he leaves scorch patterns in the shape of an X against whatever surface he passed through. The perceptual side effect is constant: he sees alternate realities as a persistent overlay, other versions of every moment bleeding through at the edges.
- **Trinity Grace** (16) develops vibration control. She can manipulate the frequency of matter at will — dampen sound into silence, amplify it into structural force, reach into the resonant frequency of an object and shake it apart or hold it still. She does not need her voice to use it. She has not used her voice since the Crash.
- **Majo Majima** (26) develops healing. Clean, comprehensive, and as far as anyone can determine, without side effects. She healed herself after the Crash, because the body does what it can. Abigail died anyway. There is no version of her powers that explains this or makes it right.
- **Sarah Florence** develops necromancy. She can reach across whatever boundary separates the living from the gone. She can summon. She can commune. She can make the shape of a person present in a room. Every time she uses her powers, she is a little less tethered to consensus reality. The psychosis begins.
- **Nadia Vasquez** (31) develops the ability to interface with electrical systems — any current-carrying infrastructure within range, she can sense and rewrite. The side effect runs in the other direction: strong feelings push a surge outward through whatever's around her. Every phone in her apartment has died at least once. The building's fuse box has been replaced twice.
- **Marcus Webb** (34) develops the ability to open portals — precise spatial doorways between any two points he can visualize clearly. The side effect is paradoxical: severe claustrophobia and agoraphobia, simultaneously. The only configuration that doesn't press on him from both sides is standing in a literal doorway, between spaces.
- **Alexander Poe** manifests last, by his own account. His stated power is electrical generation — controlled enough to be surgical, potent enough to be lethal. It is too precise too early. What the compound may actually have amplified is something else: a perception, the ability to see the architecture of situations, the particular load-bearing weight that certain relationships and truths carry. He has described this to no one. At its furthest edge, this perception reaches something it was not supposed to reach.
- **Dale Pryce** leaves Apex Haul within the month. He develops no powers. He has not given any interviews.
- **Lazaretto** reads about the Crash. He finds the buried discovery documents that Apex Haul Logistics' lawyers worked carefully to obscure. He finds the name of the person who made the calculation — margin versus equipment — and finds the same name attached to a promotion announced six weeks after the last settlement. He begins working on this systematically.
- **Sarah's necromancy** begins reaching toward Abigail involuntarily. She does not always choose this. It happens while she sleeps, while she's distracted, while she's grieving — which is most of the time. What comes back may be Abigail. It may not. Sarah cannot always tell the difference.

## Year One: Aftermath

- Survivors begin manifesting powers. Most try to suppress or hide them.
- Michelle's paranoia worsens with the development of her forcefield powers. Her music career begins to decline.
- Sarah's psychosis deepens with each use of her necromancy.
- **Diana Rice kidnaps Jackson MacDuffie** and uses her mind control powers to hold him in servitude. He is 14. She is an adult who knew what she was doing and did it because she could. His recovery will be the kind that takes structured time and professional support.
- **Privacy and Silenced Wren work together to free him.** The coordination is improvised and effective: Michelle's forcefields give them cover, and Trinity's vibration control lets them interfere with Diana's control signal at the frequency it operates on. They didn't know it would work. It worked.
- **Majo** begins operating as a hero under the name Sympathy. She later drops the name and goes simply by Majo — her own name, no costume-name required, as if the refusal to perform heroism is itself a kind of stance.
- **Therapy forms:** Majo, Privacy, Xscape, and Silenced Wren join together. **Dr. Petra Okafor** begins working with the team through a referral from the Justice Alliance.
- Dr. Okafor establishes eight chairs in a circle. She does not offer easy comfort. She offers accurate comfort, which is slower and more durable.
- **The Meridian Center for Psychological Wellness** becomes the group's therapy home.
- **Nadia Vasquez** and **Marcus Webb**, independently, begin visiting the Crash Site. They meet there. They don't talk much. They don't need to. It helps, a little, to not be the only person standing on that stretch of highway looking at a place that changed everything and got no answers back.
- **Red Lightning** and **Mosquito Man** continue operating in Detroit, at different scales, in different neighborhoods. They have developed, through friction and occasional grudging coordination, a division of labor that neither of them has formally acknowledged. Red Lightning handles the fast and the large. Mathew handles the close and the specific.

## Year One to Two: Lectric's Infiltration

- **Alexander Poe** (Lectric) presents himself as an ally who gained powers from the Crash and wants to help. He is thoughtful, personable, and credibly reluctant — someone who had been through the same event they had, who understood what they were navigating, who seemed like someone you could sit in a circle with and trust. He is provisionally admitted to Therapy. He attends sessions. He uses what he hears.
- He pursues a **black widow strategy targeting Rock Woman** — Alice Brown, who carries centuries of accumulated wealth and who has, for the first time in a very long time, begun to let herself be reached. He courts her with the full application of his charm and the benefit of knowing, from the inside, the texture of her emotional life: what she fears, what she hopes for, what Majo means to her, and therefore what leverage exists. The plan is to marry her and defraud or kill her.
- He operates as a **mole within Therapy**, feeding operational details — the MacDuffies' movements, Majo's vulnerabilities — to Dangerous and Twilight. He is the reason the campaign against Majo is as precise as it will become.
- He nearly **takes down the Justice Alliance** before being exposed. The mechanism is quiet and structural — not dramatic. He had been in rooms. He had listened. He knew which load-bearing relationships to strain.
- He is exposed. He formally aligns with Dangerous and Twilight. He is now openly a villain.
- **Imara Solis** is brought in after three of Xscape's burns destroy civilian clothing in a single week. She asks what she needs to know, takes measurements, and leaves. She begins two years of development on the fiber composite that will become Therapy's field costumes. The Majo problem takes the longest: a low-level healing aura that degrades organic materials slowly enough that no single incident looks like a cause. She catches it by charting fiber degradation across garments over time.

## Year Two: Majo's Solo Run

- Majo and Rock Woman develop their romantic relationship — slowly, which is the only way it could have happened for either of them. One who doesn't trust enough and one who trusts too much and has paid for it, finding their way toward something that actually holds.
- **Sarah Florence (Twilight) and Diana Rice (Dangerous) form an alliance** to torment Majo. Diana's mind control and Sarah's involuntary necromantic connection to Abigail are combined into something precisely calibrated to what Majo loved most and has lost. The Abigail-shaped thing that Majo experiences during this period is not fully Abigail and not fully fabricated — which is what makes it so effective. It is the genuine grief of one person, weaponized by another.
- **Lectric intensifies his pursuit of Rock Woman**, using intelligence gathered during his time inside Therapy.
- Majo is trapped in a sustained perceptual assault. She is only freed when **Silenced Wren — who has not spoken aloud since the Crash — says something out loud for the first time.** The first words Trinity Grace says are Abigail's name, because Majo kept saying it, and someone needed to say it back in a way that was real. Trinity has not spoken again since that moment. Something was needed, and she did the thing that was needed. Her vibration control has been noticeably more precise since.
- **The pie.** Sarah comes to Majo under a pretense of reconciliation. She bakes it herself — the way she always did, the way the three of them used to, in the apartment with enough of a kitchen to actually cook in. Majo recognizes the gesture in the specific, embodied way you recognize something you've known for years. She lets Sarah in. She sets out plates. They eat together. This is not stupidity and not naivety. Majo knew she was taking a risk. She chose to take it anyway, because the alternative — the kind of survival that keeps you safe by making you unable to be reached — felt wrong. She still does not want to be that. Even after everything.
- **Majo loses her eye.** The intimacy of it is the part she does not talk about. Not the injury — injuries have a clinical simplicity that makes them easier to hold. What she does not talk about is the closeness. How well Sarah knew how to be close to her. How the geography of her trust was not something she had to teach Sarah, because Sarah had already learned it, years ago, in a different life that turned out not to be as over as either of them thought.
- **Rock Woman and Majo become engaged.**
- **Imara Solis** completes the first fully functional run of field costumes for Therapy. The Trinity composite holds. She still checks it after every reported incident.

## Ongoing / Open Questions

- **Hardwire and Threshold** continue to navigate their powers independently. Neither has declared a side. Both are watching.
- **Lazaretto** is systematically targeting the Apex Haul Logistics executives responsible for the Crash and its cover-up. He has not yet killed anyone. This is a line he has drawn and examined and confirmed and redrawn, and it is still there. He has approached **Dale Pryce** and brought him a handwritten list of every name in the buried paperwork. Dale Pryce is afraid of him. Reuben considers this a misunderstanding that patience will eventually correct.
- **Lazaretto and Lectric** are in consistent, unexplained conflict that predates the current story by longer than the visible record accounts for. Neither has offered a clean account of its origin. The intersection of what both of them decline to address is narrower than it should be.
- **Meridian BioSciences** is conducting quiet research on the Crash survivors without their knowledge. [?] What have they found? What do they intend to do with it? Their claim that remaining Quantum Spin Liquid stock was destroyed has not been verified.
- **The Crash Site's anomalies are worsening.** Electromagnetic interference, perceptual distortions, temporal echo effects in which witnesses report hearing or briefly seeing fragments of the night of the Crash replaying in the area. These effects seem to worsen around the anniversary. [?] What is the residual Quantum Spin Liquid doing to the area over time?
- **Sarah Florence** remains at large. Her psychosis is deepening. Her connection to Abigail through her necromancy is unresolved. [?] Is what she summons Abigail? Does it matter?
- **Dangerous (Diana Rice)** is unaccounted for following the events of Majo's solo run. She has declined therapy.
- **Lectric's endgame is unknown.** His greed points toward accumulation. His perception points toward something that cannot be accumulated. He has not reconciled these facts. He may be trying to find a way to make the second fact wrong.
- **Mosquito Man's parents** have not yet questioned the robotics club. His mother has commented, twice, that he seems very committed to robotics.
- **Majo and Rock Woman's wedding** is ahead. Alice cannot have children — the anomaly that makes her what she is precludes it, in ways that neither science nor magic has been able to work around. Majo cannot heal this, because it is not damage. It is structure. It is Alice. Majo has sat with this, and with her own feelings about it, and arrived somewhere she did not expect: she is not grieving a future she had planned. She is grieving, a little, on Alice's behalf.
- **Imara Solis** has declined Dr. Okafor's referral. The referral remains open.
- **Trinity Grace** is present in the circle every week. She is not forgiving Michelle MacDuffie. She has not decided if forgiveness is the point. She has committed to presence, for now, and will see what comes of it.
