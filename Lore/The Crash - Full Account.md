# The Crash — Full Account

The Crash occurred at approximately 2:17 AM on a stretch of interstate highway outside the city. The road was dry. Visibility was adequate. Weather was not a factor. There was no clear mechanical reason for what happened to either vehicle — which is, in its own way, its own kind of horror. Nobody was supposed to die that night. The conditions did not call for it.

---

## Car One

Michelle MacDuffie (Privacy) was driving a rental car southbound. She had just left an industry event with Diana Rice (Dangerous), a fellow actress. In the backseat, Michelle's teenage son Jackson (Xscape) and his girlfriend Trinity Grace (Silenced Wren) had fallen asleep together during what was supposed to be a short drive. Michelle and Diana were talking in the front. Neither of them will say, afterward, what they were talking about. It doesn't seem important against what came next, and then it seems too important to share.

## Car Two

In the northbound lane, a second car was traveling at speed. Majo Majima was driving. Sarah Florence (Twilight) and Abigail Rodriguez were in the passenger seats. They had been at a late dinner. Majo was taking Abigail home first. Sarah and Abigail were arguing in the way that people argue when they love each other — gently, the kind of disagreement that would have been forgotten by morning. Nobody remembers now what the argument was about. Nobody remembers because Abigail is not alive to remember it, and the other two cannot think about it clearly.

## The Tanker

An Apex Haul Logistics tanker truck carrying Quantum Spin Liquid for Meridian BioSciences had stalled on the highway median due to a mechanical failure the driver, Dale Pryce, had reported to dispatch earlier that evening. Dispatch did not pull the route. The truck sat partially blocking both lanes — enough to matter, not enough to be visible at speed until it was too late.

Dale Pryce was still in the cab.

Michelle's car struck the tanker first, on the passenger side. The second car hit moments later, from the opposite direction. The tanker ruptured. Quantum Spin Liquid spread across three lanes of highway.

## Car Three

A third vehicle — a northbound car driven by Nadia Vasquez, a construction worker heading home after a night shift — was close enough behind Car Two that she could not stop in time. She did not strike the tanker directly. She drove into the spill. The Quantum Spin Liquid covered the front of her car and reached her through the windshield. She pulled over onto the shoulder. She helped where she could until the paramedics arrived. Nobody at the scene knew yet what the compound was or what it would do.

---

## Abigail Rodriguez

ABIGAIL RODRIGUEZ died on impact. She was 24. She was the only fatality.

Everyone else survived, in the technical sense of the word — they were alive when the paramedics arrived. What they survived into is a longer story.

---

## Alexander Randall (Lectric)

Alexander Randall (Lectric) was not in either car. He was, according to his own account, driving behind Michelle's car and stopped when he saw the collision. He was exposed to Quantum Spin Liquid through contact with the road surface while approaching the scene. He is the last person documented to have developed powers from the Crash. The mechanism of his exposure is the most disputed element of any survivor's account — most of the compound was concentrated at the point of impact, and the distance he claims to have been standing from it is inconsistent with the exposure he would have needed. Nobody has been able to prove anything. Lectric has not volunteered clarification.

---

## Aftermath

The police report lists the cause as multi-vehicle collision due to an unlighted road obstruction. The tanker's contents are documented as "industrial chemical compound, transport manifest on file." The manifest was sealed under a Meridian BioSciences confidentiality agreement and has not been made public.

Apex Haul Logistics' liability was settled out of court, with non-disclosure agreements attached to every settlement. Meridian BioSciences issued a statement about cooperating with investigators and has not discussed the incident publicly since.

The survivors mostly stopped talking to each other. Some of them started therapy. The Crash Site, the stretch of highway where it happened, has never fully returned to normal.

Dale Pryce left Apex Haul within the month. He has not given any interviews. He has no powers. He was just a man in a truck that his company should have pulled from the road, and none of what followed is in proportion to what he did or didn't do, and he knows it, and it doesn't help.
