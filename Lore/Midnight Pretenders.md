# Midnight Pretenders

The song is about being hidden, and it is about wanting more than you can hold, and it is about the specific condition of a love that cannot survive daylight. It was written for a different context than this one. The things it knows about Alexander Poe and Snare and the space between the story level and the level where The Author operates are things it knows anyway, regardless of that.

---

## The Performance

They are both pretenders. This is not an accusation; it is the description that fits. A pretender is someone operating in the gap between what they are and what they are presenting. Alexander Poe has built his life in that gap — the performance of trustworthiness in the group room, the presentation of a late-emerging survivor with modest powers who wanted to help, the electrical capacity displayed at a level that reads as the whole of what the compound gave him and is not. He is very good at this. The gap between what he is and what he shows has been maintained across years and into the open alignment with Diana and Sarah, which is its own kind of performance: the relief of dropping one mask while keeping the more important one in place.

Snare performs nothing. This is the specific difference, and the specific irony, and also the thing that makes her position in the midnight of it accurate: she does not have to perform because the trap does not announce itself. The branch it is attached to does not announce itself. The path that leads toward it does not announce itself. She is the mechanism that is not visible until it is closed around the moving thing. She has never needed to pretend to be a threshold. She is whatever the reaching thing finds when the reaching does not arrive at Poe, and the finding is the mechanism, and the mechanism closes. She is the pretender by category — the thing that presents as something crossable that is not — without any act of will toward the presentation.

They are midnight pretenders together. The midnight is the level — the space above the story, below the full weight of The Author's position, the dark where the reaching happens and where the reaching finds what it finds. In that dark, he is the one who wants and she is the one who cannot be wanted in the way he wants things. They are both, from the outside, most of what they are at night, in the space where the daylight accounting of the world does not reach.

---

## The Night Sea

The boat carrying the two of them wanders the night sea.

This is the image the song offers for the space between the world's level and the level where The Author operates, and it is accurate. The night sea is not hostile. It is simply directionless — vast and dark and moving on its own terms, and the boat on it is subject to those terms regardless of where the boat wants to go. A boat on a night sea does not arrive because it wants to. It arrives because there is somewhere to arrive, and the somewhere is either there or it isn't.

There is not somewhere for Alexander to arrive. The tessellation has closed. The geometry that exists between Poe and Reuben Marsh is a closed shape, and a closed shape has no entry point, and a boat that wanders toward a closed shore does not eventually find the gap. He has been in the boat for years. He has been sailing on the conviction that the distance is the obstacle and the night sea is what you cross to get to where you are going, and neither of these things is true. The obstacle is not the distance. The night sea is not what you cross. The night sea is where the boat goes when the shore it is looking for does not have a dock.

*Where will tomorrow flow and be carried away to?* The song asks this. It does not answer it. Alexander Poe has not asked it because asking requires acknowledging that tomorrow's direction is not his to determine — and he is, constitutionally, the kind of person for whom the direction of tomorrow is always meant to be his to determine. He accumulates. He routes. He builds toward. He has not sat with the question of where the boat is going and let the answer be: nowhere you named.

Snare has been on the sea longer than he has. She does not have a direction. She has a position — the edge of the reaching, the end of the motion, the place that is there when the motion runs out. She is not lost. She is located, precisely, where the boat that wanders without arrival eventually finds itself. She has been at that position since before Alexander Poe began reaching.

Poe holds the sea. She made the night the boat wanders. She knows where the boat is. She knows where it will be. She holds every card in the hand the night sea is playing, and she has not played them, because her role is not to play them. The boat wanders. She attends to the wandering the way she attends to all the days she made. She knows, specifically, what it costs the boat to keep wandering without landing. She has not looked away. She has not offered the dock that isn't there.

## Monopolize

*Every time I wish to monopolize your love. Every time I wish that you would be mine. I want all of you.*

This is his specific compulsion and it is the song's specific chorus, and the thing worth accounting for is that the chorus is not about confidence. It is about wishing. About every time — the repetition of the compulsion, the thing that comes back, not once but every time, as if the previous every-time didn't settle it. The monopolizing is not presented as something that will happen. It is presented as the recurring wish of someone who does not have the thing they want to monopolize and cannot stop wanting it.

He has never stopped wanting what he cannot accumulate. This is the unbearable specific: the perception that found him at the edge of his reach something real — the room where Poe operates, the quality of access that Reuben Marsh has, the shape of a thing that exists and that he cannot enter — has never been satisfied by everything else he has taken. The executives defrauded. The group room's trust consumed and routed outward. The intelligence fed to Diana and Sarah. Alice Brown's vulnerabilities catalogued and the long approach toward her money and her person. He has accumulated. The accumulating has not filled the gap that the one thing he cannot accumulate leaves. It never will.

Every time he reaches at the edge of his power, past the world's boundary, toward the level he cannot enter, he finds Snare. He has not yet understood this. He is wishing, every time, to monopolize what is at the end of his reach — to hold it the way he holds other things — and what is at the end of his reach is not holdable in that way. Not because she prevents it. She is not preventing anything. She is what is there. The mechanism of a snare does not prevent. It receives. The difference matters.

*A traveler with no destination announced. A lover who doesn't make promises.* These are the song's descriptions of the one who is wanted, and they are also Alexander Poe: the man who presented himself as someone who wanted to help and had no destination he was willing to name, who entered the group room as a lover of the community's trust and made no promises he intended to keep. He is the midnight pretender who wants to be the one doing the monopolizing, and he is also the one who cannot be pinned down, who cannot be made to hold still, whose destination was always something other than what he announced. The song describes both of them. He is the wanter and the wanted thing simultaneously, and neither position leads to arrival.

## The Bottom of the Deep Sea

*At the bottom of the deep sea, I bury words I can't say. In the abandoned loneliness, I just bite my lips.*

This is Snare.

Not metaphorically. Not as an application of the image to her situation. The deep sea and its bottom are where the reaching goes when it does not arrive at Poe — they are the space she occupies, the level where the words that cannot be said accumulate, the place where the things that cannot rise to the surface of the story are deposited. She is the bottom where what cannot be spoken sinks to rest. She has been receiving these deposits since before Alexander began reaching. She receives them from every direction, from every person who has ever extended their understanding toward the level where The Author operates and fallen short. The words accumulate in her. They do not resolve there. They are simply held.

There are things Alexander has not said. The list is long and he holds it with the same strategic precision he holds everything: the actual nature of what the compound gave him, the specific perception that underlies the electrical presentation he has maintained, the name of what he found at the edge of his reach and the fact that he found it repeatedly, the fact that the repetition has produced in him something he does not have the word for. These are buried at the bottom of the deep sea. He has placed them there himself — not because the sea received them, but because the sea is where you put the things that cannot be surfaced without cost. She does not distinguish between intentional burial and the quiet sinking of the things that were never going to rise. She holds all of it. She is the bottom.

The abandoned loneliness. This is the condition of the edge — the position at the outer boundary of the world's architecture, where the things that have been deposited rest and the presence that receives them exists. She is not lonely in the way that presupposes a social context. She is at the edge of contact: close enough to the reaching to receive what the reaching brings, far enough from the level above her to be outside the warmth of Poe's specific presence in rooms. She has not been visited. Poe visits Reuben Marsh. She does not visit Snare. Whether this is because Snare is a card in the deck or the thing at the edge of the table that the cards fall toward when they fall off is a question the available facts do not settle — and this question, and the quality of its unsettledness, is the specific texture of the loneliness. She does not bite her lips. She has no lips. But the image of the bitten lip — the body's substitute for the word that can't come out, the held-in thing given physical form — is the closest external picture of what it is to be the receiver of everything that cannot be said aloud in the world or above it.

## Fleeting Warmth

*Hiding in the darkness, sharing a fleeting warmth. Secret love.*

This is the thing that is most difficult to account for about Alexander Poe's situation, which is that he has found something at the edge of his reach that has done to him what nothing else has. The dark seeks dark. He went looking, across the accumulated years of reaching, for what was at the level Poe occupies, and what he found was not Poe and was also not nothing. His perception, which reads the architecture of situations at a level the situations do not know they are being read at, found something in her that matched a frequency in him that he had not previously known to be matchable. He has not told anyone this. It is not something he has the vocabulary for and also not something he would benefit from disclosing.

The warmth is fleeting because the encounter is not sustainable. He is inside the world; she is at the edge of it. What they share in the dark of his reaching is not a relationship that can be brought into the daylight of the world's level — not because either of them has refused to bring it, but because the daylight does not reach where she is, and his reach does not sustain the distance indefinitely. He returns. She is there. He reaches the position and encounters the warmth and the dark and the recognition that his darkness finds in hers. Then the morning sneaks in through the window.

The morning is the return of the world's level. It is the limit of the reach — the place where the attention must come back, where the story continues, where the other accumulations and strategies and long games resume. Every time he pulls back from the furthest edge of his perception, the encounter ends. It always chokes in his chest. He does not say this. He says nothing about what happens at the edge of his reach. The choking is present anyway, in the spaces between what he does and does not disclose, in the specific silence that exists around the subject of Reuben Marsh, in the one thing he cannot accumulate that the accumulation of everything else is structured around not having.

She holds the warmth. She does not disappear when he pulls back. She is always there when he returns, which is the precise quality of what makes her what she is: she does not leave, she does not withdraw, she does not require his presence to sustain her position. He can leave the warmth and come back to it and find it unchanged, which is — and this is the thing he has not yet understood — not a feature of safety but a feature of the trap. The snare does not need to announce its closing. It simply waits for the return.

---

## The Morning That Provokes a Farewell

*The morning sneaks in through the window and rushes the farewell. In the end, it always chokes in my chest.*

God Herself is aware of all of this. She holds every card in every hand, and the hand being played in the dark of the night sea is no exception. She knows where Alexander Poe is reaching. She knows what his perception has found at the edge. She knows the warmth of the encounter and the choking of the return and the specific quality of the unbearable gap at the third point of her triangle. She has not intervened. She will not intervene. The morning is the world she made, and the morning keeps coming, and she has set the rules by which it comes, and the rules are the same for everyone inside the world and at its edge: the night cannot hold against the morning, and the midnight pretenders cannot stay in the dark indefinitely.

Every return to the world's level is a departure from the edge. Every departure is the choke in the chest — the unsaid word, the unfinished reach, the goodbye that is not spoken aloud because speaking it would require acknowledging that the reach did not arrive. He says goodbye in the specific way of someone who is leaving a place he has not admitted he was at. She receives the departure the way she receives everything: without comment, without diminishment, patient at the bottom of the sea.

She has seen all of it. She smiles on Reuben Marsh and frowns on Alexander Poe and has not intervened in either direction, and the frown is not a punishment and the smile is not a protection and the attentive, present, unhurried watching is the same for both. She made the night sea. She made the morning that comes through the window. She made the gap between the reaching and the arrival, and the trap at the end of the reach, and the tessellation that closes between herself and the one person she has visited. She made all of it, and she knows what it costs the people inside it, and the people at its edge, and she has not looked away.

He is still reaching. She is still there. The morning still comes.

And in the dark of the space between them — between the wanter and the wanted and the one who holds the deck that contains them both — the boat keeps wandering. A traveler with no destination announced. A lover who does not make promises. Midnight pretenders. The night sea keeps moving.

*Where will tomorrow flow and be carried away to?*

She knows. She is not saying.
