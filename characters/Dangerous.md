# Dangerous

Dangerous is Diana Rice — actress, charmer, person who got everything she wanted and then got the ability to make sure of it, which turned out to be the worst version of that story.

She was in Michelle MacDuffie's car the night of the Crash. In the front seat, talking to Michelle while Michelle drove, while Jackson and Trinity slept in the back. Whatever they were talking about, neither of them has shared it. After the Crash, Diana's version of the afterward diverged from Michelle's so completely that it's hard to believe they came from the same night.

Her powers are mind control — specific, reliable, and deeply effective against people who are emotionally compromised, which is most people most of the time. She can rewrite what someone believes is happening, redirect what they want, make them compliant with her preferences in ways they won't recognize as coercion. She is good at this. She was probably good at a version of it before the Crash, too.

The side effect is that her own inhibitions are disabled. Not damaged, not reduced — disabled. What Diana wants, she pursues. The internal friction that most people have, the voice that says *not this, not now, not this way* — she doesn't have that anymore. This is not a personality flaw. It is a neurological consequence of the Quantum Spin Liquid interaction, and it is also not meaningfully distinct from how she presents: Diana Rice has always known what she wanted. The Crash simply removed the speed bump between wanting and taking.

She kidnapped Jackson MacDuffie — Xscape, Privacy's teenage son — and used her powers to hold him in servitude. Not briefly. Long enough that when Privacy and Silenced Wren finally found him and broke the compulsion, his recovery was the kind that takes structured time and professional support. His mother has not forgiven Diana for this. She will not.

Privacy and Wren's coordination was improvised and effective: Michelle's forcefields gave them cover, and Trinity's vibration control let them interfere with Diana's control signal at the frequency it operated on. They didn't know it would work. It worked.

Diana's later collaboration with Sarah Florence (Twilight) was more calculated. The campaign against Majo Majima required Diana's mind control to function as a delivery mechanism for something Sarah provided — the genuine shape of Abigail Rodriguez's grief, necromantically assembled, handed off to Diana to direct. Diana understood what they were building. She built it precisely. The Abigail-shaped thing that Majo experienced during that period was calibrated to Majo's specific love, and Diana did the calibration.

She also brought Alexander Randall (Lectric) into the alliance. Whether she trusts him is a question she has probably not asked herself, because trust is the kind of consideration that operates on a longer timeline than Diana typically works with.

She is not in therapy. She has been approached. She has declined.

She is at large.
