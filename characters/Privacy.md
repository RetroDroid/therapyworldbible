# Privacy

Privacy is Michelle MacDuffie. Her stage name is Michelle Jaxon. She has been famous for long enough that she thinks of fame as a condition of the environment rather than something being done to her, and the Crash was the first thing that has ever made her feel like she was about to lose it.

She was 38 at the time of the Crash. Her music career had been built over fifteen years — not an overnight ascent but a sustained, deliberate accumulation of work, reputation, and audience. She is good at what she does in the way that people who are genuinely gifted and also relentlessly disciplined are good at things. She is a performer. She has always understood that performing means managing what people see.

Her powers are invisibility and forcefields. The invisibility is clean and complete: she can render herself and nearby others entirely undetectable to the eye. The forcefields are structural — barriers she can raise between a space and anything outside it, impermeable and stable. Both powers are genuinely useful, and she understands how to use them.

The problem is the forcefields' side effect: they silence everything said behind them. Sound does not pass through. Whatever conversations happen inside a privacy field are held inside it completely, and when the field drops, nothing that was said in it carries out. This is, as a power, deeply metaphorical, and Michelle has spent a significant amount of time in Dr. Okafor's office not discussing this. The more immediate problem is the paranoia.

The Quantum Spin Liquid interaction found something in Michelle's neurological architecture and amplified it into a clinical condition. She developed severe paranoia — the pervasive, structural kind, where the person cannot identify where the paranoia begins and accurate social reading ends. Michelle is a public figure who has spent decades correctly tracking what people want from her and how they talk about her. The paranoia worked with that existing skill set and made it into a weapon against her. She became unable to go on stage without believing the audience could see through her. She became unable to hold a collaboration together. She canceled tours. She went quiet on social media and came back wrong and went quiet again. Her management described it as burnout. It was not burnout.

She is in therapy. She began therapy specifically because of her own deterioration, and she stayed in therapy because of Jackson.

Jackson MacDuffie — Xscape — is her son. He is the most important person in her life, which she does not say in interviews because she protects him, and which she says very plainly in group sessions because Dr. Okafor has established that the group is a place where things are said plainly. When Diana Rice kidnapped Jackson and held him under compulsion, Michelle's response was not grief or paralysis — it was cold and focused and thorough. She and Trinity Grace worked together in a coordination that surprised both of them and produced the outcome they needed.

Trinity Grace (Silenced Wren) is also in Therapy because of Michelle — or because of what Michelle did, before the Crash, in an accident that left Trinity mute and traumatized and afraid of her. Michelle carries this. She does not minimize it in sessions, and she does not avoid it, and she sits in the same circle as Trinity every week with the knowledge of what she cost her. Trinity is there in part because Majo and Jackson pushed for it, and in part because the alternative was leaving Trinity out of the space where the people who were connected to her trauma were doing the work of surviving it. This is not a resolution. It is a configuration everyone has agreed to try.

Michelle's stardom is returning. The paranoia is managed rather than gone, and there is a difference between managed and healed that she is learning not to collapse. She has written one song about the Crash. She has not released it. She has played it for Dr. Okafor. That was enough, for now.
