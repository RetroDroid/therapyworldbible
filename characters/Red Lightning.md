# Red Lightning

Red Lightning is a mechanically enhanced speedster from the year 2170. At 23 years old, he ran faster than the speed of light for the first time and was propelled backward through time, arriving in the present day. He did not plan this. There is no way back that he's found yet.

His real name is not publicly known — he is cautious about paradoxes, and his name is the kind of detail that could create problems. What is known: he is working with his great-great-great-grandfather James Webb, a 60-year-old retired electrician in Detroit's Corktown neighborhood, and James's granddaughter (currently 22, Red Lightning's great-great-grandmother) to build the suits that let him harness his powers safely. Family dinners are surreal. Everyone has agreed not to think about it too hard.

James Webb's home doubles as their workshop, which looks from the outside like a moderately successful auto repair shop. James has handled the revelation of his future time-traveling descendant with remarkable practicality. He keeps a separate notebook for "things I should probably not know" and does not open it often.

The containment suit is ongoing engineering. At high speeds, Red Lightning's body generates forces that his biological frame cannot sustain unaided. Each iteration of the suit has gotten better. He's on version seven.

He protects Detroit from harm. He and Mosquito Man operate in different neighborhoods and at different scales and cross paths more than either prefers. They are not enemies. They are two people who occasionally trip over each other.

He is in therapy because being displaced 150 years in time, while he has largely adjusted to functionally, is genuinely a traumatic experience — one that has no community, no framework, and no other survivors to compare notes with. Dr. Okafor was the first person to say that plainly to him, without trying to soften it or make it philosophical. It was the most useful thing anyone had said to him since he arrived. He has been in weekly sessions since.
