# Meridian BioSciences

Meridian BioSciences is a pharmaceutical research company that commissioned the transport of Quantum Spin Liquid in the weeks before the Crash. They were synthesizing the compound as part of a classified internal research program studying quantum-state interactions with biological tissue — specifically, whether stable quantum matter could be introduced into cellular processes to produce targeted therapeutic effects.

Their findings, at the time of the Crash, were inconclusive. The compound behaved unpredictably in controlled laboratory conditions. They had not yet tested it on living subjects. They were transporting it to a partner facility where that phase of testing was scheduled to begin.

After the Crash, the program was indefinitely suspended. Their official statement at the time acknowledged only that one of their transport contracts had been involved in a traffic accident and that they were cooperating with investigators. They did not disclose what was being transported. The non-disclosure agreements attached to Apex Haul's settlements ensured that the compound's origin was not publicly connected to the company for several years.

Meridian BioSciences is not a villain organization. They are a company that believed they were doing legitimate science, outsourced the transport to the lowest bidder, moved faster than their ethics review could keep up with, and did not tell the trucking company what they were actually carrying. The catastrophe that resulted was not intentional. It was, in the estimation of several people who have looked at the documents, entirely preventable.

They have conducted quiet research into the Crash survivors since the incident — carefully, at a distance, without the knowledge or consent of those being studied. This research has not been published. It is not clear who in the company knows about it or what they intend to do with what they've found.

Their claim that all remaining Quantum Spin Liquid stock was destroyed after the accident has not been independently verified. The people who would need to verify it cannot get a straight answer about who to ask.

Do not confuse with the Meridian Center for Psychological Wellness. Dr. Okafor has asked people to stop making that joke.
